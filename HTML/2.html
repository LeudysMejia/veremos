Rural Community Tourism represents an opportunity for income generation and development for rural communities located near tourist attractions and that still do not benefit economically from visitors to the area.Different interventions to promote rural community tourism already existing in the Latin American region have certain weaknesses that affect a lack of appropriation of marketing by local populations and tour operators, as well as limitations regarding sustainability. At the same time, there are limitations that are still present in other public and private actors in terms of promoting community tourism and the potential of Public-Private-Community Alliances in this area.

Community rural tourism is increasingly being used by development agencies and NGOs as a development strategy, as well as by the local public sector in terms of land, resource and heritage management. This publication is part of the series ‘ROUTES: The commitment to inclusive tourism in Latin America’. Thanks to her we want to disseminate and publicize the RUTAS program, its main characteristics and the results achieved at the end of the intervention. With the aim of being able to help other organizations to develop community rural tourism programs.
